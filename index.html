About Me

Hello, my name is Oscar Rodriguez. I recently completed my studies at California Community College, specifically Mount San Antonio College (Mt. Sac). I am excited to announce that I will be transferring to UC Irvine for the Fall 2023 quarter as a junior, with a major in Software Engineering B.S. My educational journey at Mt. Sac has of my involvement outside the classroom was amazing. I had the privilege of serving as the Team Captain for Rust and partnering with Doomba v2, representing MTSAC2 CORE, during the VEXU Robotics Tipping Point season (2021-2022). In this role, I took part in competitive events, demonstrating our skills and teamwork. Additionally, I dedicated my time to support local high and middle school events at Mt. Sac, where I volunteered as a referee, inspection team member, and match queuer. These roles provided deserving teams with the opportunity for those to qualify for a place at the VEX Worlds in Dallas, Texas. Our efforts not only opened doors for aspiring young engineering students but also enabled us to compete in our respective division at USC, ultimately securing our spot at the Worlds and Cal State Northridge.

At the pinnacle of the VEXU season, I was honored to assume the role of an embedded tutor and teaching assistant for the Introduction to Engineering Programming Concepts and Methodologies class at Mt. Sac, working under the guidance of Professor Kenneth Ash. In this capacity, I organized peer-led group study sessions twice a week, providing valuable academic support beyond regular classroom hours. During lab sessions, I assisted students in debugging code, ensuring proper Arduino and breadboard setups, and clarifying questions related to coding and sensors. This enriching experience has deepened my passion for engineering and education, motivating me to pursue a rewarding career in Software Engineering.
